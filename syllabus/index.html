Course Description

ART 329 Web Design. An introduction to the tools and techniques associated with building webpages and sites using professional-level software. Prerequisite: a grade of “C” or higher in ART 110 or ART 120. (3 s.h.)

Textbook

Head First Web Design by Ethan Watrall & Jeff Siarto. ISBN-10: 0596520301 | ISBN-13: 978-0596520304 | Publication Date: December 30, 2008 | Edition: 1.

Course Objectives

Students will develop an understanding of:

  • Adobe Dreamweaver
  • Firefox & Firebug
  • web design and theory
  • visual metaphor, theme, storyboards
  • information architecture
  • web color theory
  • navigation techniques
  • accessibility
  • HTML markup (doctype, <head> tags, headings, block tags, inline tags)
  • CSS styling (selectors, properties, values, tips & hacks)
  • HTML5 & CSS3 introductory topics

Teaching Info

Teaching Methods

Online material. Important material from the text and outside sources will be covered. Students will be required to read and study all online lecture material before meeting in class. Students will also be required to post information on the my.usf site for this course. Point deductions will result from failure to so.

Lecture/discussion. There will be regular discussion in the classroom. Lecture will cover the details of the reading and will not be posted online.

Classroom demonstrations/lecture review. Demonstrations and lecture review will be done during class time. It is important that online lectures are read prior to meeting so that students will be able to ask questions and understand the material presented.

Reading/Assignments. Weekly reading and other various assignments will be given to reinforce material in the text.

Quizzes. Short online quizzes will be required by students before each class. Quizzes will be available at the same time lectures are posted, and will expire at the start of class time on Friday.

Participation. Student participation and level of class/online participation and attendance may affect your final grade.

Course Policies

Missed Classes. The student is responsible for obtaining material distributed on class days when he/she was absent. Please do so by contacting a classmate who was present.

Assignments. All assignments are due at the beginning of class on the date due. Late submission of assignments will be assessed a penalty of 10% per day. No exceptions are made.

Incomplete Policy. Students will not be given an incomplete grade in the course without sound reason and documented evidence as described in the Student Handbook. In any case, for a student to receive an incomplete, he or she must be passing and must have completed a significant portion of the course.

Grading Info

Grading

Grades are reported at midterm and at the end of the semester. A midterm grade will give you an indication of your performance in the course for the first half of the semester but will not become part of your permanent record. Your final semester grade will become part of your permanent record.

USF uses an expanded grade point system. In addition to the 4.0 for an A, 3.0 for a B, 2.0 for a C, and 1.0 for a D, for example, an A- translates as 3.7 quality grade points, and a C+ as 2.3 quality grade points.

Total points will be computed as follows:

  • Attendance: 140 Points (14 @ 10 points each)
  • Quizzes: 130 Points (13 @ 10 points each)
  • Assignments: 240 Points (12 @ 20 points each)
  • Final Project: 300 Points
  • Total: 810 Points

Student Evaluation

Projects will be graded on the basis of: application of design concepts or, in other words, your grasp of the subject matter, evidence of fulfilling the problem objectives in an appropriate fashion, creativity and the quality of your design, effort, attention to detail, punctuality. Projects must be present before critiques to receive full credit.

Redoing Projects

Depending on the circumstances, instructor may allow students to redo one assignment for the semester. Student must visit with instructor for approval, and present a plan on he/she will improve their grade.
